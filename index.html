Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

By considering the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making, one is able to create designs that are easily accessable, user friendly, and visually appealing. Without using these principles a design can lack what is needed to be considered well rounded.

2. Leverage Design Principles.


Through readings, in class descussions, and using different design platforms we are able to take different design principles and apply them to our designs. Each project either uses a neq design principle that we learn in class or adds more design principles to work with. This allows us to create more appealing and accessable designs.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

In class we work often with groups to discuss our projects, work, and ideas. Through this we are able to help one another solve problems, take feedback, and elevate our designs. This environment is extremely benefitial due to the fact that you are able to learn from your peers, their designs, all while being extremely positive.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Practicing revision is stressed in this class. By being able to workshop your designs with fellow classmates, you are able to go back to your work and change anything that might be needed to make the design more accessable and visually appealing. Revision across all media forms allows us to learn from our mistakes.

5. Learn to present your work.


Throughout the course we are given the opportunity to present our work to recieve feedback. This is exremely beneficial due to the fact that during presenting you are able to hear many opinions and ideas that you can apply to your design to make it even better than it was before. Learning to present also teaches one self confidence, which can be applied outside of the classroom and the workforce.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Thoughout the second half of this course, we used different platforms to create our designs. The class also read numerous articles and text that stengthened our knowledge in regards to rhetoric and ethics in acts of making. In the client project, we learned how to use rhetoric and applied ethics while working with an outside organization.

2. Leverage Design Principles.


Different design principles were used for these four projects. In the 2 page spread design, we learned how to create and copy a magazine spread using Adobe. We also applied our readings and new knowledge to the client project.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Workshops are a fundemental part in this course as described before. Students are encouraged to work with other classmates as well as the professor to create work. Students also had the opportunity to work with a clien which allowed them to see how a brand, business, or organization comes together to compose digital media.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Throughout the second half of the course students were able to revise their designs and media so they can improve and learn. This option is available for all designs as well as the portfolio.

5. Learn to present your work.


The class has been presenting in small groups to showcase their work and gain insight from other classmates. We also share all designs through discussion posts, which allows students to see other peoples work, gain new ideas, and improve their own understanding of the material in this class.
